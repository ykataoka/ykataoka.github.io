Prius Challenge
Overview
Prius Challenge was the racing competition about maximizing mpg for Prius by data-driven strategy. This involves both data science(theory) and real driving skills(practice). I applied both two different machine learning approaches to identify the best strategy to achieve higher mpg. The analysis revealed, for example, where to use 'neutral'/'drive' or 'EVmode(on/off)'. As a result, our team got the award of 'Best MPG by Machine Learning'! Plus, we achieved 83mpg(w/o penalties), while Toyota dream team did 86mpg. Yeah, we were so close and I can say data-driven approach worked! This competition was held at Sonoma Raceway by Toyota Research Institute in Mar 3rd, 2017. Our team was composed of me(data scientist) and 4 other members of great drivers!
Problems
Q1. How do we identify best strategy to maximize mpg? Given the dataset, it is required to identify how use inputs (throttle, brake, gear and EV-mode)
Q2. How does data scientist deliver the strategy to drivers? This is not only data analytics but also teamwork. Delivering the result to driver is key part.
(Sonoma Raceway has tough uphill and downhill; 47[m] height. This makes this challenge hard.)
Solution
1. Comparison between high and low performance
Applying clustering technique to gps information, I have divided the race track into 150 pieces. Then, I visualized the average and variance of the 4 inputs(gear, brake, throttle, ev-mode) using high-performance dataset (top 30% mpg data) and low-performance dataset (Bottom 30% mpg data). This visualization helps driver to undersntand the strategy of how they use 4 inputs to get higher mpg.
2. Understanding remarkable point
Using 600 features (4inputs x 150 locations) and mpg data as the label, supervised learning is applied. Then, the feature importance is applied to identify the important inputs which highly affect mpg. In other words, they are the remakable points that drivers specially consider.
Result
It was an honor to recieve the prize of "Best MPG by Machine Learning" out of 20 teams! Our data-driven strategy was truly acknowledged by Toyota Research Institute. As for competition result, we achieved 83mpg on average for 9 laps(42:29). Including unfortunate penalties, the score came down to 79mpg based on the regulation. In the end, we ended up 4th position among 20 teams. However, yeah, we did great job! We were so close to Toyota dream team(86mpg) who are the most knowledable people about Prius.
Our team got ABC7 news' attention. My name was on TV! Here is the source.

My lifelong speaker, maybe
This time, I made speaker, the simple model of bass reflex. Making speaker means here is to design a box to maximize the performance of a speaker unit. This work was great and I still love them: design, texture and, of course, the sound quality. The color and speaker unit have been 'aged' and will be classical until I die. :D Now, I need to protect them from my baby's poking. But even if it is broken by accident, I am sure I will repair them and get more affection to it.
The steps
Here is the summary of the steps
  1. Decide the speaker unit
    2. Depending on the limitation of your space and the requirement of sound quality and your budget, you may want to decide the speaker unit first. If needed, tweeter should be added for better performance for high frequency sound. In Japanese, this place is very helpful. You may want to Akihabara to get feedback from the most knowledgable people. Koizumi Musen
  2. Design the box
    3. A bass reflex system (also known as a ported, vented box or reflex port) is a type of loudspeaker enclosure that uses a port (hole) or vent cut into the cabinet and a section of tubing or pipe affixed to the port. (from wikipedia) Thus, the size of the enclosure(bos), the hole affect the quality of the sound. This should be designed depending on the specification of the speaker unit that you chose. In Japanese, this site may help to design the enclosure(box).
  3. Go to home center and get materials
    4. There are various woods. Every woods have different characteristics. Stiff is said to be better, but I chose the wood by the texture and my budget. You may want to pay attention to the direction of the grain. Do not forget to get the acoustical material, which should be attached to insider of the speaker. Plus, you need to get wiring cable and terminal for it.
  4. Assemble the materials and paint
    5. You just need to assemble them. I am not writing details here, but just create whatever you want. If you make any mistake, that is part of your memory and it will become the reason why you feel affection, maybe.
  5. Test(aging)
    6. Some people talk about aging. To me, it sounds like urban legend... The idea of the aging is that the more you use the speaker, the better quality sound you can experience. Some people play music for several days, which makes the speaker unit adapts the design of the enclosure you designed.
    However, I personally do not think my sense of hearing quality is good enough to distinguish very slight but important difference of music...
Automatic Loose Ball Detection by ball trajectory analytics
Overview
I love basketball. I have played basketball since I was 6 years old. I joined to MIT Sloan Sports Analytics Hackathon hosted by ESPN in Mar.2017. The theme of the hackathon was "Measure the unmeasureable in basketball" such as teamwork, mentality, leadership and heart. Yeah, sounds very tough. This is specifically hard task as a data science task, because there are no ground truth of these factors... For this hackathon, I decided to focus on 'loose ball detection'. In basketball, loose ball is the moment you can see player's fighting spirits. Not only that, chasing loose ball has chance to 1. excite audience, 2. inspire teammates and 3. change momentum of the game. Conventionally in the given dataset by ESPN (as far as I thoroughly surveyed), the loose ball has not been recorded unless it is related to foul. Thus, finding loose ball automatically would be helpful to understand player's latent power of mentality. (Since this is one hackathon, I only spent 24~48 hours for this project in total.)
